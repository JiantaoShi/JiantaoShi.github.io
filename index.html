Welcome

My name is Jiantao Shi. I studied biology at Huazhong University of Science and Technology in China. I received PhD degree from Shanghai Institutes for Biological Sciences, Chinese Academy of Science, in 2010, where my research focused on identification and annotation of genome-wide binding sites of transcription factors. I joined the Hide Lab at The Harvard T.H. Chan School of Public Health in 2012, where I worked with Francois Mercier from David Scadden's Lab to identify the key signaling pathways driving aggressiveness of leukemia stem cells. I also worked with Rudolph Tanzi on 1000 genomes of Alzheimer's disease to identify rare non-coding mutations. At Dana Farber Cancer Institute, I am interested in modeling cancer progression using computational and mathematical methods.
